Introduction

The Chinese Remainder Theorem in Mathematics states that if one knows the remainders of the Euclidean division of an integer n by several integers, they can be used to determine the unique remainder of n's division by the product of these other integers, provided that the n and the divisors are pairwise coprime (no two divisors share a common factor other than 1).

CHINESE REMAINDER THEOREM

The Chinese Remainder Theorem (CRT), which is a method for reducing modular computations with high moduli to comparable calculations for each of the (mutually co-prime) modulus components, was first described by the Chinese mathematician Sun Zhu.

Application of the Chinese Remainder Theorem



> The Chinese remainder theorem is frequently used for computing with large integers because it enables replacing a computation for which one knows a bound on the size of the result by numerous comparable computations on tiny integers.

> In order to prove Godel's Incompleteness Theorems, a Godel numbering for sequences has been developed using the Chinese Remainder Theorem.

> The range ambiguity resolution techniques used with medium pulse repetition frequency radar can be seen as a special illustration of the Chinese remainder theorem.
